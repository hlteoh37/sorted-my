How to Send a Parcel — Step by Step

  1. Pack your item securely
    Use a rigid cardboard box for fragile items. Wrap items in bubble wrap or foam padding. For documents, use a padded envelope or place sheets in a rigid envelope. Double-tape all seams. Loose or poorly packed items are not covered by compensation if damaged.
  2. Write the correct address on the outside
    Write clearly in block capitals. Include the full Malaysian address format (see below). Stick a return address label on the back in case it cannot be delivered.
  3. Go to a Pos Malaysia outlet or authorised agent
    Find your nearest outlet via poslaju.com.my or the MyPOS app. Many minimarkets (99 Speedmart, Guardian, etc.) are authorised Pos Laju drop-off points. You can also schedule a pickup for larger volumes.
  4. Declare the contents and value at the counter
    Tell the counter staff what you are sending and the approximate value. For insurance/compensation purposes, this declaration matters — undeclared items receive minimal compensation if lost.
  5. Choose your service and pay
    The counter staff will weigh your parcel and tell you the rate. Pay by cash or card. You will receive a consignment note (CN) with your tracking number.
  6. Track via poslaju.com.my or the MyPOS app
    Enter your CN number at poslaju.com.my to see real-time status. The MyPOS app also supports push notifications when status changes.

Correct Malaysian Address Format

Using the wrong address format is one of the most common reasons parcels are delayed. Follow this structure:

[Recipient Full Name]
[Unit/House Number], [Street Name],
[Taman/Area Name],
[Postcode] [City],
[STATE]
Malaysia

Example — landed house:

Ahmad bin Ali
No. 12, Jalan Mawar 5,
Taman Bunga,
47500 Subang Jaya,
SELANGOR
Malaysia

Example — apartment/condo:

Lim Mei Lin
Unit B-12-3, Block B, Vista Condo,
Jalan Ampang,
50450 Kuala Lumpur,
WILAYAH PERSEKUTUAN
Malaysia
Common mistakes: Missing postcode, putting city before postcode, omitting the state name, or writing an incomplete street name. These cause sorting errors and delays.

Tracking Your Parcel

Online Tracking

  • Website: Go to poslaju.com.my → Track → Enter your consignment number (CN number from your receipt)
  • MyPOS app: Download from App Store or Play Store. Enter CN number for status updates with push notifications.
  • SMS tracking: Send your CN number to 33300 (standard SMS rates apply)

Prohibited & Restricted Items

  • Cash and currency — not permitted via regular post; use money order or bank transfer
  • Lithium batteries (loose) — must be installed in devices; loose batteries have special requirements
  • Aerosols, liquids >100ml (international) — restricted for air shipments
  • Perishable food — only allowed with special packaging; contact Pos Malaysia first
  • Sharp items — must be securely wrapped and declared
  • Dangerous goods — flammable, corrosive, or explosive items are prohibited
Sending food overseas? Many countries have strict biosecurity rules. Undeclared food items can cause your entire parcel to be confiscated or returned at your expense.

Lost or Damaged Parcel — What to Do

  1. Check tracking first
    Go to poslaju.com.my and check the last status. If it shows "Held at post office" and you didn't receive a notice, go to the designated outlet with your IC and CN number.
  2. Wait 5 working days from the expected delivery date
    Pos Laju's service standard is next-day within Peninsula, but delays happen. If the tracking shows no movement for 5 working days after posting, proceed to file an enquiry.
  3. File a formal claim via the Pos Malaysia website
    Go to poslaju.com.my → Customer Service → Enquiry/Complaint. Provide your CN number, posting date, sender details, and recipient details. You should receive a response within 7–14 working days.
  4. Escalate to MCMC if unresolved
    If Pos Malaysia does not resolve your complaint within a reasonable time, file a complaint with MCMC (Malaysian Communications and Multimedia Commission) at aduan.mcmc.gov.my.
Compensation limits: Pos Laju — up to RM300 for lost items (if value was declared). Standard registered post — up to RM300. Unregistered post — minimal compensation. Always use Registered or Insured Post for valuable items.

Pro Tips

  • Use the authorised drop-off agents — 99 Speedmart and other agents accept Pos Laju parcels 7 days a week, often with longer hours than post offices. No queuing.
  • Weigh your parcel at home first — use a kitchen scale. Pos Laju charges by 500g increments; knowing the weight lets you anticipate the cost and avoid surprises at the counter.
  • Photograph your parcel before dropping off — a photo showing the contents and packaging is your best evidence if a damage claim is needed.
  • Print a QR label via the app for faster service — the MyPOS app lets you create a booking and generate a QR code, which the counter scans directly. Faster than handwriting.
  • For Sabah/Sarawak deliveries, add 1–2 buffer days — the stated delivery standard is next-day for Peninsula to Peninsula, but East Malaysia routes take 2–4 business days in practice.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the correct address format for Malaysia?
Line 1: Recipient name. Line 2: Unit/house number and street. Line 3: Taman or area name. Line 4: Postcode + City. Line 5: State. The postcode must come before the city name. For example: "47500 Subang Jaya, Selangor."
How much does Pos Laju cost?
Pos Laju rates start from around RM7–8 for up to 500g within Peninsular Malaysia. Rates increase by weight and zone (Peninsula to Sabah/Sarawak is significantly higher). Check current rates at poslaju.com.my as they are updated periodically.
My Pos Laju parcel is delayed or lost. What do I do?
Check tracking at poslaju.com.my first. If no movement for 5+ working days, file a claim via the Pos Malaysia website (Customer Service > Enquiry) or call 1300-300-300. Compensation is up to RM300 for Pos Laju with declared value. Claims must be filed within 30 days of posting.
Can I send cash through Pos Malaysia?
Cash should not be sent through regular post. For money transfers use bank transfer, DuitNow, or a postal money order. For valuable items, use Insured Post with a declared value — compensation is available if lost or damaged, up to the declared amount.
How do I collect a missed delivery from the post office?
Pos Malaysia will leave a delivery notice with the outlet address. Bring your IC and the notice to the specified post office. Parcels are held for 7 working days — if uncollected, the parcel is returned to the sender.
