How progressive tax works in Malaysia: You don't pay the top rate on all your income — only on the portion that falls in each bracket. For example, if your chargeable income is RM72,000, the first RM5,000 is taxed at 0%, the next RM15,000 at 1%, the next RM15,000 at 3%, the next RM15,000 at 6%, and only RM2,000 at 11%.

💡 Tips to Reduce Your Income Tax

  • Max out your EPF voluntary contribution (VC) — up to RM4,000 is tax-deductible.
  • Claim lifestyle relief up to RM2,500 for books, internet, gym, and computers — keep receipts.
  • Don't forget SOCSO and EIS contributions — up to RM350 is claimable.
  • If you have dependants, claim spouse, child, and parent relief — these add up quickly.
  • Medical expenses for serious diseases and for parents are claimable (separate limits apply).
  • Contribute to private retirement schemes (PRS) for an additional RM3,000 relief.
  • Life insurance and education insurance premiums are deductible up to RM3,000.

🙋 Frequently Asked Questions

What is chargeable income?

Chargeable income is your total income minus all eligible tax reliefs and deductions. This is the figure that the progressive tax rates are applied to — not your gross salary.

Does this include zakat or tax rebates?

This calculator shows your base tax before zakat offsets or rebates (e.g., RM400 individual rebate for chargeable income below RM35,000). If eligible, subtract those from the tax payable shown.

When is the tax filing deadline for YA 2025?

For salaried employees (Form BE), the deadline is typically 30 April 2026. Self-employed (Form B) is 30 June 2026. e-Filing via MyTax opens in March.

What if my income is below RM34,000?

If your annual income (before reliefs) is RM34,000 or below, you are generally not required to file a tax return. However, if you have been registered before, you still should file.
